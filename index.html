Education and Human Devlopment

My name is Yamileth Sanchez and I am a first year at the University of California Riverside. I am 18 years old and Mexican. I am the eldest in my family and I have two younger siblings in highschool. I love to dance, I like staying busy. In my free time I enjoy being outdoors and working out as well as spending time with family. My current interests are majoring in Education and Human Development with a concentration in learning and behavioural studies. I also plan to minor in Spanish. Ever since I was a little girl, I knew that I wanted to work with children, especially as a first generation. Some of the barriers I had to face with my parents was first the language barrier. My parents couldn't communicate their concerns with my educators because of the language barrier presented. They heavily relied on translators to assist their needs. Thus, one of my goals as a student is to obtain a Bachelor’s in Education and Human Development. I want to work with students who are double categorized low-income and first generation.

I want to work in dual immersion to be able to teach students in two languages. Establishing more dual immersion programs has always been something that has captivated my interest and I would like to improve in my community. The dual immersion program is a unique educational program designed to develop bilingualism and biliteracy in English and Spanish. This is a voluntary program. Instruction is provided by high trained and specialized teachers. Some benefits of the dual immersion program are new studies showing that a multilingual brain is nimbler, quicker, better able to deal with ambiguities, resolve conflicts, and even resist Alzeimher’s disease and other forms of dementia longer. I also want to study abroad in the future in Spain to be able to take some Spanish courses when possible.
